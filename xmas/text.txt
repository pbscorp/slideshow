QM1: In which country did the tradition of decorating a Christmas tree originate?
CM1: A) France, B) Germany, C) Norway, D) England
AM1: B) Germany. The tradition began there in the 16th century, originally using evergreen trees decorated with candles.
QM2: What was the first year Christmas was declared a federal holiday in the United States?
CM2: A) 1776, B) 1812, C) 1870, D) 1901

AM2: C) 1870. President Ulysses S. Grant signed the legislation on June 28, 1870, to establish Christmas as a federal day off.
QM3: Which U.S. state was the first to recognize Christmas as an official holiday in 1836?
CM3: A) Alabama, B) Massachusetts, C) New York, D) Virginia
AM3: A) Alabama. It became the first state to declare Christmas a legal holiday in 1836, decades before it became a federal holiday.
QM4: The first artificial Christmas trees were made in Germany using what unusual material?
CM4: A) Shredded paper, B) Dyed goose feathers, C) Pine needles glued to wire, D) Dried moss
AM4: B) Dyed goose feathers. These "feather trees" became popular in Germany during the late 19th century as a response to deforestation.
QM5: What is the traditional name of the "Christmas log" cake?
CM5: A) Panettone, B) Stollen, C) Bûche de Noël, D) Galette des Rois
AM5: C) Bûche de Noël. This French dessert dates back to the 19th century and represents the actual Yule log families burned on their hearths.
QM6: In the 1990 classic Home Alone, where is the McCallister family going on vacation?
CM6: A) London, B) Paris, C) New York City, D) Rome
AM6: B) Paris. The film was a massive success in 1990, becoming the highest-grossing live-action comedy of all time until 2011.
QM7: Who played the character of Buddy in the 2003 film Elf?
CM7: A) Steve Carell, B) Will Ferrell, C) Jim Carrey, D) Adam Sandler
AM7: B) Will Ferrell. Since its release in 2003, Elf has become a modern holiday staple, grossing over $220 million worldwide.

QM10: Which department store was the setting for the 1947 film Miracle on 34th Street?
CM10: A) Gimbels, B) Bloomingdale's, C) Macy's, D) Saks Fifth Avenue
AM10: C) Macy's. The 1947 film features the real Macy's Thanksgiving Day Parade and the flagship store in Herald Square, NYC.
QM11: Which Christmas song was the first ever broadcast from space in 1965?
CM11: A) Silent Night, B) Deck the Halls, C) Jingle Bells, D) White Christmas
AM11: C) Jingle Bells. Gemini 6 astronauts Wally Schirra and Tom Stafford played the song on a harmonica and bells on December 16, 1965.
QM12: In the song "The Twelve Days of Christmas," what is given on the seventh day?
CM12: A) Seven geese a-laying, B) Seven swans a-swimming, C) Seven lords a-leaping, D) Seven maids a-milking
AM12: B) Seven swans a-swimming. The song was first published in England in 1780 as a rhyme in a children's book.
QM13: What is the best-selling Christmas single of all time according to Guinness World Records?
CM13: A) Mariah Carey, B) Bing Crosby, C) Wham!, D) Brenda Lee
AM13: B) Bing Crosby. "White Christmas," released in 1942, has sold an estimated 50 million copies worldwide.
QM14: In "Frosty the Snowman," what are Frosty's eyes made of?
CM14: A) Coal, B) Buttons, C) Pebbles, D) Blueberries
AM14: A) Coal. The song was first recorded in 1950 by Gene Autry and the Cass County Boys following the success of "Rudolph."
QM15: What kind of pudding is mentioned in the classic carol "We Wish You a Merry Christmas"?
CM15: A) Plum pudding, B) Bread pudding, C) Figgy pudding, D) Yorkshire pudding
AM15: C) Figgy pudding. This 16th-century English treat was a cake-like bread containing figs and honey.
